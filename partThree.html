Why is the Electoral College Used

Throughout this process of researching and finding out more information on the electoral college I learned a lot about why we as a country decided to use the electoral college as a means of picking the President of the United States. This research experience has given me a new point of view on America’s democracy. I believe that having a good understanding of the history behind our electoral college will help us continue to shape our modern democracy. Throughout researching information on the electoral college, I realized just how outdated this system is.

The Divide

I found in the research I conducted, most people’s reasoning to keep the electoral college is that the popular vote would allow big states such as California or New York to control the presidential election. Since the electoral college remains part of the Unites States Constitution, many conservatives are strongly against abolishing the electoral college. On the other side many liberals have pushed heavily to abolish the electoral college with hundreds of proposed amendments that offer various alternatives. I found it very interesting that this process has ben around since the constitution was written and has not been revised for over one-hundred and fifty years. This process has shined a light on a topic that had always confused me and made me appreciate the entire process way more.

Negatives of the Electoral College

This information has really inspired me to contribute more to our democracy.
